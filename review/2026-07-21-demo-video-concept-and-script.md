# Demo Video — Concept & Script (the "new mental model" cut)

*Written 2026-07-21. Supersedes the framing in the v2 plan; the v2 production/shot notes still apply for logistics. Under 3 min, public YouTube, your voice VO + real screen. English lines are kept verbatim — say them exactly.*

---

## 1. The one-sentence goal

Not: *"We built this app."*
But: **"We made a new hypothesis about how people learn a language, and we proved it with GPT-5.6."**

Judges watch dozens of demos a day. They forget features. They remember a **new way of seeing**. The aim of the first 10 seconds is the feeling: **"Wait… that's actually true."**

## 2. Why this reframe

- This project is not an English app. It is an idea that **flips how language learning works**, and the app exists to prove it.
- So the video should feel like **proving one theory**, not touring an app.
- The word **"English" comes as late as possible.** The real subject is **thought transfer**: expressing the thoughts you already have, in another language.

## 3. The claim, and its basis (this is the part that must be defensible)

The linguistic claim **must** be in the video — but as an **observation**, never as "we propose a theory." A paper-style claim fails in 3 seconds; a first-person observation lands.

**The observation (your philosophy):**
> You don't think in English. You already know how you think. The hard part is expressing those same thoughts in another language.

**The basis / why it's true (your evidence, defensible):**
- When you can take the **expressions and thought-flow you already use every day** and learn to say *those* in the target language first, progress is **faster, less frustrating, and more fun** — because you are translating your own mind, not memorizing someone else's templates.
- **Cognitive compression:** people don't generate new thoughts each day. They repeat **similar thoughts in similar structures**:
  - `Example → Lesson`
  - `Question → Answer`
  - `Story → Reflection`
- That structure **largely survives a language change.** Vocabulary and grammar change; your way of organizing ideas mostly doesn't.
- So the fastest bridge is a role model who **already organizes ideas the way you do** — not the "best" English speaker.

**How it stays defensible (honesty):** it is framed as an **observation and a hypothesis the product tests and makes checkable** (every match shows verified evidence), not a proven scientific law. That honesty is a strength, not a weakness.

## 4. How to frame GPT-5.6 (why, not what)

Do **not** say "GPT does 3 steps" (that is a tech talk). Say **why GPT is needed**:
> There isn't a formula for communication style. GPT-5.6 reasons over your transcript, builds a structured communication profile, verifies every behavioral claim, and only then recommends creators.

And the sharpest line:
> GPT-5.6 doesn't analyze your English. It reasons about how you organize ideas.

**Framing guardrail:** the video presents **GPT-5.6 as the product's core reasoning engine** and **Codex as the build engineer**. It does not enumerate development-time tooling — that is a separate story and not what judges score.

## 5. How to frame Codex (an engineer, not a code writer)

> This wasn't built by asking Codex to write an app. We used Codex as another engineer.

Show, fast: a **PR, a test, an architecture note, a prompt, an agent run**. Then one hard decision solved together:
> Together we redesigned the reasoning pipeline until recommendations became consistent.

## 6. The signature scene (put this in — it is what makes it memorable)

On the results screen, **Jay Shetty** appears. Then cut:
- 10 seconds of his real YouTube clip, with captions.
- Then the user's own transcript.
- **Split screen, left/right.**
- One line on screen:
> They don't use the same words.
> They use the same way of thinking.

This single scene explains the whole project.

## 8. Rules to hold while shooting
- **Say "English" as late as possible.** The subject is thought transfer.
- **Observation, not theory.** First person. No "we propose."
- **Show, don't explain.** 70%+ is the real app running, one clean journey.
- **Open and close are the same idea, flipped.** That bookend is the memory hook.
- **The split-screen scene is non-negotiable** — it is the proof in one shot.
