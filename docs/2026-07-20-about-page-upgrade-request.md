## Upgrade 1 — Draw the creator discovery network (turn the chip chain into a real diagram)

**The concept (corrected):** the role models are **real English vloggers**, a living pool that keeps
growing. The two-sided story is NOT "learners become role models." It is **discovery for creators**:
when a learner matches a vlogger, that vlogger gets found by exactly the person who wants to learn
from them, so a small vlogger gains a new way to be discovered through the way they talk.

**Problem today:** the card is only a row of text chips and it shows the wrong idea (a learner
graduating into a role model). Redraw it as the discovery network above.

**Fix:** replace the chip chain with a small **two-sided network diagram**, still badged `Coming
soon`, reusing the avatar-gradient nodes and blue connecting lines. Keep the graphic style, change
the meaning.

Diagram spec (simple and legible):
- **Left: learners** — 3 to 4 small muted avatar dots, labeled `Learners`.
- **Right: creators** — a few larger avatar-gradient nodes, labeled `Creators` (or `Vloggers`). This
  is the growing pool.
- **Blue lines** = `matched` by how you talk, learner to vlogger.
- **Show discovery, not graduation:** highlight one creator node with several learner lines arriving
  and a small badge `found by learners who talk like them`. Include one **small/new** creator node
  getting its first match, to show small creators being discovered.
- **Remove** any "learner becomes a role model" curved arrow and any "new learners find them"
  loop-back.
- Legend: `matched` (line) + a `gets discovered` marker on a creator node.
- Caption: `Small creators get found by learners who already talk like them, and every new learner makes the matches better.`

Copy for the section:
- Heading: `A new way for real creators to be found.`
- Frame line: `The role models are real English vloggers, and that pool keeps growing.`
- Body: `When a learner matches a creator, that creator gets in front of exactly the person who wants to learn from them. For a small vlogger, that is a new way to be discovered, not through ads, but through the way they talk.`
- Network-effect line: `More learners means better matches and more creators found. A bigger pool of creators means a better match for every learner.`

Optional polish: a soft scroll-reveal (reuse `lrm-rise`). Keep it on the dashed `Coming soon` card.

## Upgrade 2 — Add an honest "Good to know" section (differ + cautions)

A calm, confident section (not red warnings). Two compact blocks. Placement: after the reasoning /
pull-quote area, or as its own section before the final CTA. Give it a plain eyebrow like
`GOOD TO KNOW` and a heading like `What this is, and what it is not.`

**Block A — How this is different (sharpen by contrast).** Three short lines, category-based (no need
to name brands):
- `Apps that score your accent tell you how native you sound.`
- `Soundalike tools tell you which famous voice you resemble.`
- `We do neither. We look at how you build your thoughts, then point you to real people who talk a similar way, so copying them feels natural.`
Lay these as three small rows or a tight three-column strip, the third one emphasized (blue) as "us".

**Block B — When this is a poor fit (the honest caution = the concept, made precise).** A soft card,
plain and friendly:
- `Talk the way you normally would, in your own language. Loose and unscripted is perfect.`
- `A rehearsed script read out loud, or written English typed in, is a poor fit. A polished performance hides how you really talk, and how you really talk is the one thing we listen for.`
- `This is a learning-fit suggestion, not a score on your voice or your English.`
Tone: helpful, confident, not defensive. Use a calm icon (info or a small check), not a warning
triangle. This block explains, in plain words, *why native and natural* matters, which is exactly the
concept, so it reads as clarity, not apology.

*(Why this helps: naming the limits honestly is a trust signal, and it doubles as the clearest
one-paragraph explanation of what the product actually reads, your present, natural speaking voice.)*
