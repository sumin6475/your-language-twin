# Positioning — Adversarial Review

*2026-07-19. A skeptical growth/brand + hackathon-judge review of the positioning, used to shape
the brand brief and why-panel spec in this folder. Kept for the record.*

---

## Biggest weaknesses found

1. **"Stop shadowing strangers" fights the wrong enemy.** The match result is itself a stranger, so
   the line quietly contradicts the product. It also assumes a pain the user does not feel yet.
   Dropped.
2. **"Thinks like you" over-claims.** We measure how people talk, not how they think. It invites the
   killer question ("do you actually measure thinking?") and reads like a personality quiz.
   Downgraded to "talks the way you do".
3. **The core value is hard to grasp in one screen, and "why record my native language?" is the
   single most confusing step.** It must be answered on screen 1. Added the why-native line.
4. **"Shadow / shadowing" is insider jargon** the whole frame leaned on. Replaced with copy / imitate
   / practice with everywhere in user-facing copy.
5. **The three "real system" signals were weighted equally.** They should not be. The evidence
   (shared traits) signal is worth more than the other two combined. Re-weighted 80/10/10.

## Believability verdict

"Match by how you talk" lands as **gimmicky** by default (sits next to zodiac apps). It becomes
**credible** only by showing checkable receipts: specific things the learner and creator both do,
audible in both clips, in plain words. Under-claim the promise, over-deliver in the evidence panel.

## Chosen lines

- Headline: **Learn English from someone who already talks the way you do.**
- Subhead (contrast, does competitor work): **Most apps help you sound like a native speaker. We
  find the native speaker who already sounds like you.**

## Judge lens (one strongest + one risk per criterion)

- **Technological Implementation** — Show: the cross-language trick is real ("you spoke Korean, we
  matched you by how you talk"). Risk: it reads as a thin "ask GPT who this person talks like".
- **Design** — Show: the two clips / traits side by side, one "aha" screen. Risk: soulmate-style
  match %, or failing to answer "why native language?".
- **Potential Impact** — Show: everyone is told to imitate native speakers, nobody is told *who*.
  Risk: reads as niche-of-a-niche toy unless the two-sided hint is done well.
- **Quality of Idea** — Show: the inversion (analyze the language you are NOT learning, to learn the
  one you are). Risk: sounds like a generic recommendation engine if the "your own style" hook is
  not front and center.

## Jargon replacements (now enforced in the brief)

shadow → copy / repeat after · thinks like you → talks the way you do · rhetorical style → the way
you talk · hedging → softens with "kind of" · "creator, coming soon" → "Do you make videos in
English? ... get found".
